# Function to display basic statistics
def display_statistics(x, y, x_label, y_label):
    correlation = x.corr(y)
    print(f"Correlation between {x_label} and {y_label}: {correlation:.2f}")
    print(f"Mean of {x_label}: {x.mean():.2f}")
    print(f"Median of {x_label}: {x.median():.2f}")
    print(f"Standard Deviation of {x_label}: {x.std():.2f}")
    print(f"Mean of {y_label}: {y.mean():.2f}")
    print(f"Median of {y_label}: {y.median():.2f}")
    print(f"Standard Deviation of {y_label}: {y.std():.2f}")
    print("\n")

Conclusion:
1. **Change in Uninsured Rate vs. Employer Health Insurance Coverage (2010-2015):**
   - The correlation between the uninsured rate change and employer health insurance coverage is weakly negative.
   - This suggests that states with a larger decrease in the uninsured rate tend to have slightly lower employer-provided insurance coverage, but the relationship is not strong.

2. **Change in Uninsured Rate vs. Marketplace Tax Credits (2016):**
   - The correlation is positive but moderate, implying that as the change in the uninsured rate increases, states may be providing more marketplace tax credits.
   - This indicates that some states are addressing insurance access through marketplace subsidies as the uninsured rate decreases

3. **Medicaid Enrollment Change vs. Marketplace Tax Credits (2013-2016):**
   - There seems to be a moderate positive correlation between Medicaid enrollment change and marketplace tax credits.

Overall Insights:
   - The analysis reveals some moderate correlations between these variables, suggesting that changes in health insurance coverage, both through employer insurance and public programs like Medicaid, are linked to tax credits and marketplace subsidies.
   - However, none of the relationships are overwhelmingly strong, indicating that while these factors influence one another, other variables and policies likely play a significant role in shaping health insurance dynamics.
